# Methodology — Target Company Research (DeepThought Assignment)

## 1. Objective
The objective of this research was to identify and shortlist 25 Indian manufacturing companies (specifically in the Pune region) that match the “Federer Company” profile defined by DeepThought — i.e., growth-oriented, technically driven, and focused on building in-house capabilities rather than outsourcing.

The focus was on **industrial sensors, instrumentation, and automation MSMEs**, as these companies align strongly with DeepThought’s “Scientific Execution” model.

---

## 2. Scope Definition

### Geography
- Primary focus: Pune, Maharashtra  
- Reason: Pune is a strong industrial hub for instrumentation, automation, and engineering MSMEs.

### Industry Segments Selected
- Industrial sensors
- Process instrumentation
- Automation systems
- Precision measurement systems
- Mechatronics-based industrial solutions

---

## 3. Research Approach

### Step 1: Initial Company Discovery
Companies were identified using:
- Google search (industry + Pune keywords)
- B2B directories (IndiaMART, trade listings)
- Industrial automation supplier listings
- Sensor and instrumentation ecosystem mapping
- LinkedIn company search (manufacturing + Pune filters)
- Known industrial clusters and MSME networks

---

### Step 2: Data Collection
For each company, the following information was gathered:
- Company name and website (if available)
- Product portfolio (what they manufacture)
- Industry category
- Estimated revenue band (based on public data / assumptions)
- Location confirmation (Pune-based operations)
- Growth indicators (hiring, expansion, certifications, etc.)

---

### Step 3: Filtering Framework (Federer Model)

Each company was evaluated using 6 criteria:

1. **Manufacturing capability** — whether they produce in-house
2. **India presence** — operational/manufacturing base in India
3. **Differentiation** — presence of technical depth, IP, or specialization
4. **Technical leadership** — founder/MD technical background
5. **Growth sector alignment** — industry tailwinds (Make-in-India, automation, etc.)
6. **Active growth signals** — hiring, expansion, certifications, revenue growth

---

## 4. Scoring Method

Each criterion was scored as:
- Weak = 0
- Moderate = partial weight
- Strong = full weight

Final **Federer Score (out of 100)** was calculated using weighted scoring:

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Manufacturing capability | 10 |
| India presence | 5 |
| Differentiation | 25 |
| Technical leadership | 20 |
| Growth sector | 20 |
| Growth signals | 20 |

---

## 5. Classification System

Companies were categorized as:

- **A (80–100): Strong Federer Fit** → High priority targets  
- **B (60–79): Probable Fit** → Good potential, minor caveats  
- **C (40–59): Borderline** → Requires further validation  
- **D (<40): Not ICP** → Excluded from final list  

---

## 6. Data Cleaning Process

During final preparation:
- Duplicate companies were removed
- Distributor-only or non-manufacturing firms were excluded
- Large corporates (>500 Cr revenue or PE-owned firms) were filtered out
- Only MSMEs with active operational signals were retained
- Final list was reduced to 25 high-quality companies

---

## 7. Key Observations

- Pune has a strong cluster of **sensor, flow measurement, and automation MSMEs**
- Many companies operate in niche instrumentation segments with global-level precision capabilities
- Majority of strong-fit companies show engineering-led leadership (founders with technical backgrounds)
- Growth signals are strongest in automation and precision sensor segments

---

## 8. Limitations

- Revenue estimates are approximate due to limited public MSME financial data
- Some growth signals were inferred from secondary sources (job postings, websites)
- Private company internal strategy data was not accessible

---

## 9. Conclusion

This research successfully identifies 25 companies that closely match the Federer ICP profile for DeepThought. The dataset emphasizes manufacturing depth, technical leadership, and growth potential within Pune’s industrial automation ecosystem.

The methodology ensures a structured, repeatable approach that can be scaled to larger datasets (e.g., 1000+ companies) using automation and AI-assisted sourcing in future iterations.
